

## Executive Summary

A controlled A/B experiment was conducted to evaluate the effectiveness of a new onboarding and activation campaign. Users were randomly assigned to either the existing onboarding experience (Control group) or the new onboarding campaign (Treatment group).

The objective was to determine whether the new onboarding experience should be launched to all users based on improvements in user conversion while maintaining a positive customer experience.

---

# North Star Metric

The selected North Star Metric was **Paid Conversion Rate** because it directly measures the percentage of users who become paying customers after completing the onboarding process.

Improving this metric contributes directly to recurring revenue and long-term business growth.

---

# KPI Tree Summary

The KPI Tree identified Paid Conversion Rate as the primary business objective.

The main drivers supporting this metric were:

- User Acquisition
- Onboarding Success
- Revenue Quality

The experiment also monitored several guardrail metrics:

- Refund Rate
- Support Ticket Rate
- Average Engagement Score
- Days to Convert

These metrics ensured that conversion improvements did not negatively affect customer experience.

---

# Experiment Summary

The Treatment group outperformed the Control group across several important business metrics.

Key improvements included:

- Higher Landing Page Visit Rate
- Higher Trial Start Rate
- Higher Onboarding Completion Rate
- Higher Paid Conversion Rate
- Higher Average Engagement Score
- Faster Conversion Time

These results indicate that the new onboarding experience improved user progression through the conversion funnel.

---

# Hypothesis Test Interpretation

The hypothesis test compared the Paid Conversion Rate between the Control and Treatment groups.

The statistical test showed evidence that the Treatment group achieved a higher Paid Conversion Rate than the Control group.

Based on the selected significance level, the experiment supports rejecting the null hypothesis and suggests that the Treatment onboarding experience provides a meaningful improvement in user conversion.

---

# Guardrail Analysis

Although the Treatment group demonstrated stronger conversion performance, several guardrail metrics were also evaluated.

Positive Findings:

- Higher user engagement
- Faster conversion
- Higher overall revenue per user

Risks Identified:

- Slight increase in refund requests
- Higher support ticket rate
- Lower average revenue per converted customer

These risks should continue to be monitored after deployment but are not severe enough to outweigh the observed business benefits.

---

# Segment-Level Insights

Segment analysis showed that Treatment performance remained generally consistent across Region, Device Type, and Traffic Source.

No major segment demonstrated a significant decline that would prevent rollout.

Future experiments may further optimize onboarding for individual customer segments.

---

# Final Recommendation

## Recommendation: Launch

The Treatment onboarding campaign should be launched because it produced a meaningful improvement in Paid Conversion Rate while maintaining acceptable performance across the evaluated guardrail metrics.

The increase in support tickets and refund requests should continue to be monitored after deployment, but the overall business impact of the new onboarding experience is positive.

---

# Risks and Limitations

This analysis is based on the available experiment data.

Some external factors that may influence customer behaviour, such as seasonality, competitor actions, pricing changes, and long-term customer retention, were not included in the experiment.

Additionally, statistical association does not automatically imply causation beyond the experimental conditions.

---

# Next Steps

- Launch the Treatment onboarding experience.
- Continue monitoring refund rate and support ticket rate.
- Track long-term customer retention and lifetime value.
- Perform additional experiments to optimize onboarding for different customer segments.
- Re-evaluate performance after rollout using ongoing KPI monitoring.
