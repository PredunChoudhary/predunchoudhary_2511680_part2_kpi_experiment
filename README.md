## Business Problem

The company has introduced a new onboarding and activation campaign for new users. Users were randomly assigned to either the existing onboarding experience (Control group) or the new campaign experience (Treatment group).

The leadership team must decide whether the new onboarding campaign should be launched to all users.

This decision affects business leaders, product managers, marketing teams, customer success teams, and future users of the platform.

The primary objective is to improve the paid conversion rate while maintaining healthy user engagement and revenue.

Before recommending a full launch, it is important to ensure that improvements in conversion do not negatively impact customer experience. Therefore, guardrail metrics such as refund rate, support ticket rate, engagement score, and days to convert must also be evaluated.

The final recommendation should be based on experiment results, hypothesis testing, supporting KPI analysis, and guardrail metrics rather than relying on a single performance indicator.

## North Star Metric

### Selected North Star Metric

The North Star Metric selected for this experiment is **Paid Conversion Rate**.

**Formula:**

**Paid Conversion Rate = Number of Users Converted to Paid ÷ Total Number of Users**

### Why This Is the North Star Metric

The primary objective of the onboarding and activation campaign is to increase the number of users who become paying subscribers. Paid Conversion Rate directly measures the effectiveness of the onboarding experience in converting new users into paying customers. An increase in this metric contributes directly to recurring revenue, customer acquisition success, and long-term business growth, making it the most appropriate measure of experiment success.

### Supporting Metrics

Several additional metrics were evaluated to understand user behavior throughout the conversion funnel. These metrics support the North Star Metric but do not directly represent the overall business objective.

* **Landing Page Visit Rate** – Measures how many users reached the landing page.
* **Trial Start Rate** – Indicates user interest in trying the product.
* **Onboarding Completion Rate** – Measures how successfully users complete the onboarding process.
* **Average Revenue per User (ARPU)** – Evaluates the revenue generated from all users.
* **Average Revenue per Converted User** – Measures revenue generated by paying customers.
* **Average Engagement Score** – Reflects how actively users engage with the product after onboarding.

These supporting metrics help identify where improvements occur within the user journey but are not the primary indicator of business success.

### Connection to Business Growth

Improving the Paid Conversion Rate increases the number of paying subscribers, resulting in higher recurring revenue and improved customer lifetime value. A successful onboarding experience that converts more users into paying customers supports sustainable business growth and improves the return on marketing and product investments.

### Risks of Optimizing Only the North Star Metric

Optimizing Paid Conversion Rate without monitoring other business metrics may create unintended consequences. For example, aggressive onboarding tactics could increase conversions while also increasing refund requests, support tickets, or reducing user satisfaction and engagement.

To ensure balanced decision-making, the following guardrail metrics should also be monitored throughout the experiment:

* Refund Rate
* Support Ticket Rate
* Average Engagement Score
* Average Days to Convert

Monitoring both the North Star Metric and these guardrail metrics ensures that improvements in conversion are achieved without negatively affecting customer experience or long-term business performance.

## KPI Tree Summary

The KPI Tree was developed to illustrate how the North Star Metric, **Paid Conversion Rate**, is influenced by multiple operational drivers throughout the customer journey.

Three primary KPI drivers were identified:

1. **User Acquisition**, measured through Landing Page Visit Rate and Trial Start Rate.
2. **Onboarding Success**, measured through Onboarding Completion Rate and Average Engagement Score.
3. **Revenue Quality**, measured through Average Revenue per User and Average Revenue per Converted User.

To ensure improvements in conversion do not negatively affect customer experience or long-term business performance, three guardrail metrics were monitored:

* Refund Rate
* Support Ticket Rate
* Average Days to Convert

This KPI Tree provides a structured framework for evaluating experiment performance while balancing business growth with customer satisfaction.

## Data Cleaning and Preparation

The experiment dataset was reviewed before analysis to ensure data quality.

The following validation checks were performed:

- Checked for missing values.
- Verified duplicate user IDs.
- Compared user counts between Control and Treatment groups.
- Validated binary variables to ensure they contained only 0 and 1 values.
- Reviewed revenue values for potential outliers.
- Compared segment distributions across Region, Device Type, Traffic Source, and Plan Type.

No major data quality issues requiring data removal were identified. Any revenue outliers were retained because they may represent legitimate high-value customers.
